How Can You Help The Environment?

“He that plants trees loves others besides himself.” —Thomas Fuller

There are plenty of ways to help the environment, from just turning off your lights when they’re not in use to going out in your community to clean up trash.

One way to reduce the harmful impact of waste is to recycle. There are many items that you think are trash but are actually recyclable.

1. Computers (many electronic stores have take-back programs)

2. Cell Phones and PDA’s (here is more information on recycling all electronics)

3. Batteries and rechargeable batteries (learn more about Call2Recycle)

4. Inkjet and toner cartridges (check with your local office supply store or school)

5. Apple® devices (here is a great resource for selling and recycling Apple Products)

6. Greeting cards (use them for art projects or other creative projects, or recycle them with your paper)

7. All types of glass jars can be recycled as long as they had consumables in them

8. Eyeglasses can be recycled

9. Pizza boxes can be recycled with cardboard if they are not greasy or soiled with food

10. Shaving cream (watch for the recycling symbol and favor the plastic packaging)

11. Plastic silverware (check if your curbside program accepts #6, otherwise just wash and reuse yourself)

12. Toilet paper rolls, paper towel rolls, oatmeal canisters (it’s cardboard!)

13. Reusable food storage containers (i.e. Ziploc® brand

14. Plastic shopping bags (many grocery stores will take them back, but try to bring your own bag instead)

15. Packaging for things like tissues, cosmetics, toothbrushes, q-tips, and other hygienic products

16. Deodorant packaging (even some spray cans)

17. Aluminum foil if it is clean (also screw top lids from wine bottles, foil trays, etc.)

18. Aerosol cans can be recycled with steel if they are empty

19. Old medications (check with your local pharmacy or law enforcement agency)

20. Milk and juice cartons (even when they appear coated!)

The little things at home

  • Avoid taking cars and carpool whenever possible
  • Turn it off. Artificial lighting accounts for 44 percent of electricity use in office buildings. Make it a habit to turn off the lights when you're leaving any room for 15 minutes or more. Same goes for electronics; switch off power strips and unplug electrical devices when you're not using them.
  • Use cold water. Using cold water can save up to 80 percent of the energy required to wash clothes. Choosing a low setting on the washing machine will also help save water.
  • Switch to e-billing. In the United States, paper products make up the largest percentage of municipal solid waste, and hard copy bills alone generate almost 2 million tons of CO2. Save paper by signing up for e-billing.
  • Recycle. You can recycle plastic bottles, paper, electronics and batteries, among other items. Learn how to properly dispose of or recycle these products and reduce consumer waste.
  • Dial it down. Moving your thermostat down just two degrees in winter and up two degrees in summer could save about 2,000 pounds of carbon dioxide per year.
